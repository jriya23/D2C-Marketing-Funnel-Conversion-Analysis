# D2C-Marketing-Funnel-Conversion-Analysis

🚀 Project Overview

This project analyzes the customer journey of a Direct-to-Consumer (D2C) e-commerce business to understand how users move through the marketing funnel—from website visits to completed purchases.

The objective is to identify conversion bottlenecks, evaluate marketing channel performance, understand customer behavior, and provide actionable recommendations to improve conversion rates and revenue.

🎯 Business Problem

The company receives significant website traffic, but only a small percentage of visitors complete a purchase.

This results in:

Lost revenue opportunities
Low marketing ROI
High customer drop-off across the funnel
Inefficient allocation of marketing budget

The business needed a data-driven approach to understand where customers abandon the purchasing journey and which channels contribute most effectively to conversions.

📌 Project Objectives
Analyze user progression through the marketing funnel
Measure conversion rates at each funnel stage
Identify major drop-off points
Evaluate marketing channel performance
Compare conversion behavior across devices
Analyze new vs returning customer performance
Assess campaign effectiveness
Generate business recommendations to improve conversions

📊 Key Insights

1️⃣ Major Funnel Leakage

A significant percentage of users drop off between:

Product View → Add to Cart

This indicates potential issues with product page experience, pricing perception, or purchase intent.

2️⃣ Returning Customers Convert Better

Returning customers demonstrated:

Higher conversion rates
Better purchase intent
Greater revenue contribution
3️⃣ Email Marketing Outperformed Other Channels

Email and Organic Traffic generated:

Higher conversion rates
Better customer quality
Stronger revenue contribution

compared to Paid Advertising channels.

4️⃣ Desktop Users Converted More Frequently

Desktop users showed:

Higher engagement
Better checkout completion rates
Greater purchase likelihood

than Mobile users.
